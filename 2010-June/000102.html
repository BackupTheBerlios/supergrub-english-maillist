> I downloaded Super GRUB2 Disk 1.30 and burned it to a CD, but it does not boot.

A few other people also reported that 1.30 wouldn't boot on certain
machines. None of those people (that tried it and reported back) had
the problem with 1.98s1, and I haven't had any reports of 1.98s1 not
booting.

> Does grub2 have to be installed for it to boot?

No, Super GRUB2 Disk is completely self contained. There are two
options that require GRUB2 to actually be installed and those are
"Detect any GRUB2 configuration file (grub.cfg)" and "Detect any GRUB2
installation (even if mbr is overwritten)". In the next release of
Super GRUB2 Disk there will be a similar option for finding and using
a menu.lst file from a grub legacy installation. All of the other
options are completely independent of the bootloader used by installed
operating systems.
