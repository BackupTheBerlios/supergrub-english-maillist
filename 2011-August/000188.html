El 12/08/11 01:35, J.V. escribió:
> How do I use the SuperGrub disk to restore my CentOS system to a
> bootable state?
>
> I had CentOS installed and did this:
>
> #grub-install /dev/sda1
>
> then after reboot, I have a black screen with a single white blinking
> cursor, I wish to get my OS back.
>
> I tried using he CentOS orginal boot media but that packaging of grub is
> broken.
> I tried Knoppix but that gave error messages as well, so now trying
> SuperGrub disk and am getting grief there as well.
>
> ---
> I looked at the supergrub wiki page, but the instructions have menu
> items that are no longer present in the latest supergrub disk (1.98)
>
> any ideas?
>
> Can this functionality that was so helpful be put back into the
> superdisk? It took effort to take it out.

Basically it depends on the filesystem that it is in sda1.
If I have not forgotten it xfs filesystems do not have a partition boot 
record and thus get corrupted when doing that.

You should try to boot with Super Grub2 Disk and then run as root:

   grub-install /dev/sda

and see what happens.
